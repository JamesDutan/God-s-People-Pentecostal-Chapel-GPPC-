  • Last Sunday's Sermon

    Love God, Love People

    Matthew 28:16-20
    We should be moved by love and compelled by compassion for other people.
    To love God truly is to obey Him wholeheartedly. In Matthew 28:16–20, Jesus commissions His disciples to go, make disciples of all nations, and teach them to obey everything He commanded. This Great Commission is not driven by duty alone, but by love—love for God who has all authority, and love for people who need hope, truth, and salvation. When we love God, our faith cannot remain private or passive. It pushes us outward, beyond comfort, beyond familiarity, compelling us to reflect God’s heart to a broken world. Obedience becomes an act of love, and mission becomes a natural response to knowing who God is.

    Loving people, therefore, is not optional for the believer; it is evidence of our love for God. We should be moved by love and compelled by compassion for others—seeing people not as interruptions, but as souls of eternal value. Compassion makes us notice pain, injustice, and spiritual need, and love urges us to act. As we go into our families, workplaces, campuses, and communities, we carry the assurance Jesus gave: “I am with you always.” When love becomes our motivation and compassion our driving force, God uses ordinary lives to accomplish extraordinary purposes.
